Flag flies against the wind

It is popularly believed that the temple flag always flutters opposite to the wind direction. Devotees consider it a miracle, but there is no verified scientific evidence proving this phenomenon. The temple's sacred flag, called the Patita Pavana Bana, is changed every day by temple servitors who climb the temple without modern safety equipment. Many people believe the flag always flutters opposite to the wind direction, considering it a divine miracle. However, there is no scientific study confirming this claim.


Sudarshan Chakra appears to face everyone

The large chakra (wheel) on top of the temple seems to face an observer from any direction in the city. This is generally explained as an optical illusion created by its design and placement. The Sudarshan Chakra mounted at the top of the temple is about 20 feet high and weighs nearly a ton. It is designed so that it appears to face the viewer from any direction in Puri. Experts believe this effect is due to its symmetrical design and the angle at which it is installed.


Sea sound disappears inside the entrance

Many visitors say the sound of the nearby ocean is clearly heard outside but becomes much quieter after entering through the main gate. This is a well-known local phenomenon, though different architectural and acoustic explanations have been suggested. The temple is located close to the Bay of Bengal, yet many visitors notice that the sound of the waves becomes much quieter after entering through the main gate, known as the Singhadwara. Some believe this is miraculous, while others suggest that the temple's architecture and surrounding walls create an acoustic effect.


Mahaprasad rarely goes to waste

Temple tradition holds that food prepared each day is sufficient for devotees without significant shortage or excess. Devotees see this as a divine blessing, while others attribute it to centuries of experience in estimating demand. Every day, thousands of devotees receive Mahaprasad, the sacred food offered to Lord Jagannath. Temple tradition says the quantity prepared is always sufficient, regardless of the number of visitors, and very little is wasted. This is regarded by devotees as a blessing and by others as the result of centuries of experience in estimating demand.


Wooden idols are periodically replaced

The idols of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are made of wood and are replaced during the sacred Nabakalebara ritual, usually after specific intervals determined by the lunar calendar. The transfer of the sacred essence is performed in secrecy according to tradition. Unlike most Hindu temples that have stone or metal idols, the deities of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are carved from sacred neem wood. During the special Nabakalebara ritual, held at specific intervals based on the lunar calendar, new idols are created and the sacred essence is ceremonially transferred to them in a secret ritual.
